## What Is Isolated Phase Bus?

Isolated phase bus, often abbreviated as **IPB**, is a type of metal-enclosed bus system in which each phase conductor is housed in its own individual metallic enclosure. It is widely used in power plants for high-current connections between generators and transformers.

## Typical Applications

- Generator to main transformer connections
- Generator to unit auxiliary transformer connections
- Generator to excitation transformer connections
- PT and lightning arrester branch circuits
- Medium- and large-capacity thermal power plants
- Hydropower and other utility power projects

## Key Features

### High current capacity
Wetown IPB systems are designed for demanding generator outlet applications and can support a wide range of current ratings for different project scales.

### Excellent electromagnetic shielding
Each phase is enclosed separately, which helps reduce electromagnetic effects outside the enclosure and improves the thermal performance of surrounding steel structures.

### Reliable enclosure protection
The enclosure is designed for harsh environments and can achieve **IP65** protection, making it suitable for outdoor installation and challenging site conditions.

### Intelligent monitoring integration
The system can be equipped with monitoring units for temperature, humidity, pressure, leakage, and other key operating conditions.

### Anti-condensation protection
Wetown can provide air circulation drying and micro-positive pressure solutions to reduce the risk of internal moisture and condensation.

Condensation inside enclosed bus systems can affect insulation performance and long-term reliability, especially in humid climates or during start-stop cycles. To address this, Wetown can provide air circulation drying and micro-positive pressure protection.
