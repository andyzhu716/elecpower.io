## What Is Isolated Phase Bus?

Isolated phase bus, often abbreviated as **IPB**, is a type of metal-enclosed bus system in which each phase conductor is housed in its own individual metallic enclosure. It is widely used in power plants for high-current connections between generators and transformers.

Compared with other busbar configurations, IPB offers strong electromagnetic shielding, high current-carrying capability, and dependable performance in large generator applications. It is particularly suitable for power station projects where safety, stability, and long-term operation are critical.

## Anti-Condensation Solution

Condensation inside enclosed bus systems can affect insulation performance and long-term reliability, especially in humid climates or during start-stop cycles.

To address this, Wetown can provide:

### Air circulation drying system
A controlled drying solution that reduces internal moisture and lowers relative humidity inside the bus system.

### Micro-positive pressure protection
When humidity conditions are stable, the system can maintain slight positive pressure to reduce the entry of external moisture and dust.

### Integrated protection logic
Drying, positive pressure, sensing, and alarm functions can be combined into a coordinated operating strategy to support safer plant operation.
